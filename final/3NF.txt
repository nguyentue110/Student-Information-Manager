========================================
3NF — Third Normal Form (Premium Version)
========================================

Requirement for 3NF:
A relation is in 3NF if:
    1. It is already in 2NF
    2. It has NO transitive dependency
       (i.e., no non-key attribute depends on another non-key attribute)

At this stage, we verify:
    • Each table’s PK
    • Whether any attribute depends on another non-key attribute
    • Whether decomposition is needed

----------------------------------------
1) STUDENTS
----------------------------------------
Primary Key:
    StudentID

Attributes:
    FirstName, LastName, DOB, Gender, Address, Phone, Email,
    EnrollmentYear, Major

Analysis:
    • All attributes depend directly on StudentID.
    • No attribute depends on another non-key attribute.
      (Example: Email does NOT determine Address, Major does NOT determine Year, etc.)

Conclusion:
    STUDENTS satisfies 3NF.


----------------------------------------
2) SUBJECTS
----------------------------------------
Primary Key:
    SubjectCode

Attributes:
    SubjectName, Credits

Analysis:
    • No attribute depends on another non-key attribute.
    • Everything depends only on the PK.

Conclusion:
    SUBJECTS satisfies 3NF.


----------------------------------------
3) LECTURERS
----------------------------------------
Primary Key:
    LecturerID

Attributes:
    LecturerFirstName, LecturerLastName, LecturerEmail, Office

Analysis:
    • All attributes depend solely on LecturerID.
    • No transitive dependencies.
    • Email does not determine Office, etc.

Conclusion:
    LECTURERS satisfies 3NF.


----------------------------------------
4) CLASSES
----------------------------------------
Primary Key:
    ClassID

Attributes:
    SubjectCode (FK)
    LecturerID (FK)
    Semester
    Year
    MaxCapacity

Analysis:
    • SubjectCode → SUBJECTS table
    • LecturerID → LECTURERS table
    • But inside CLASSES itself, no attribute depends on SubjectCode or LecturerID.
      (e.g., MaxCapacity is not determined by SubjectCode or LecturerID)

Conclusion:
    CLASSES satisfies 3NF.


----------------------------------------
5) ENROLLMENTS
----------------------------------------
Primary Key Options:
    Option A: EnrollmentID (surrogate key)
    Option B: Composite key (StudentID, ClassID)

Attributes:
    Grade, GradeLetter, Note

Analysis:
    • All non-key attributes depend on the PK.
    • No attribute depends on another non-key attribute.
      (e.g., Grade does NOT determine Note, GradeLetter does NOT determine Grade)

Conclusion:
    ENROLLMENTS satisfies 3NF.


----------------------------------------
FINAL RESULT
----------------------------------------
All relations from the 2NF design satisfy 3NF.

No further decomposition is required.
The schema is now fully normalized and ready for transformation
into the final relational model (ERD → schema.sql).
